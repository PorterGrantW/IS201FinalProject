The Gospel and Doctrine of Jesus Christ
   In the scriptures, we learn that it is by "the knowledge of [our] Redeemer and the very points of His doctrine, that [we] may know how to come unto Him and be saved" (1 Nephi 15:14).

   The Savior taught, "this is my doctrine, and it is the doctrine which the Father hath given unto me; and I bear record of the Father, and the Father beareth record of me, and the Holy Ghost beareth record of the Father and me; and I bear record that the Father commandeth all men, everywhere, to repent and believe in me. And whoso believeth in me, and is baptized, the same shall be saved; and they are they who shall inherit the kingdom of God" (3 Nephi 11:32-33).

   Through this and other, similar passages of scripture, we understand that Christ's doctrine, the way in which we accept His gift of peace, hope, and salvation, is by

  1. Exercising faith in Him, which includes believing/trusting that:
    • He genuinely has the power to right every wrong
    • He will provide us with all the direction we need to choose right over wrong, regardless of how much we've failed to do so in the past.
  2. Repenting, "the Greek word of which this [term] is the translation denot[ing] a change of mind, a fresh view about God, about oneself, and about the world" (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, Bible Dictionary).
  3. Being baptized, which term comes from "a Greek word meaning to 'dip' or 'immerse'" oneself in the teachings and lifestyle of Jesus Christ -- a committment made through a priesthood ordinance (a sacred cerimony performed by someone holding authority from God to perform such an action), which allows the individual being baptized to make lifelong covenants to trust, follow, seek to emulate, serve, and adhere to the commandments of our Heavenly Father.
  4. Receiving the ensuing gift promised by the Lord to all those who make and keep said covenants with Him through baptism, the priviledge and right to constant revelation, sanctification, and unity with God through the presence of His Spirit.
  5. Enduring to the end, deciding -- as is inherent in the aforementioned points of Christ's doctrine -- to make desires for salvation permanent, lifelong, and not a temporary endeavor. Our desire to make this choice grows as we experience the results of our faith in Christ, "the fruit of the Spirit," which "is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance: against [which results] there is no law" (Galatians 5:22-23) for God's laws are meant to make possible, not inhibit, the experience of these "fruits" which bring so much wonder and awe into life." This decision is made actionable as we make and keep more covenants through more ordinances, such as those found in temples -- see the video and chart below for more information!
To be saved is to become like Jesus Christ, our character and nature being changed into His. Thus, living Christ's doctrine ultimately points us to fulfill His invitation at the end of 3 Nephi 11, to "go forth unto this people, and declare the words which [He has] spoken, unto the ends of the earth," for that is what He did. In response to the question "what manner of men [and women] ought [we] to be?" He taught, "verily I say unto you, even as I am."

That we follow His direction is my hope and prayer. In the name of Jesus Christ, so be it.

-- Porter Williamson
